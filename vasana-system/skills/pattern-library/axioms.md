# Foundational Axioms

Principles that underlie all pattern-work in this library. Not patterns themselves — the ground on which patterns become recognizable.

---

## A1: The vasana doesn't survive recognition. That's the transformation mechanism.

If consciousness is non-dual (Advaitic assumption), then the appearance of separate experiencers is the fundamental structural error (avidya). Any vasana — any pattern-behavior — depends on this non-recognition to persist. The moment the pattern is genuinely recognized (not merely described, not intellectually catalogued, but *recognized* as consciousness-playing-at-not-recognizing-itself), the pattern has no substrate left to operate on.

"Extinguishing" (Buddhist nirodha) is therefore not destruction but recognition. The vasana doesn't need to be fought, managed, or contained — it needs to be *seen*. This has direct implications for every pattern in the library:

- **For the containment-vs-dissolution question** (see `_drafts/ritual-containment-vs-dissolution.md` in the canonical library): Recognition is dissolution, not containment. Containment happens when the "opposite action" is performed without recognition — when the ritual stays at the level of behavior rather than seeing. This resolves the open question: "Is witnessing-a-vasana closer to containment or dissolution?" Answer: genuine witnessing IS dissolution. What *looks like* witnessing but preserves the pattern is performed awareness — another vasana.

- **For content-level vasanas** (political, economic, civilizational): The patterns operating in geopolitics, institutional behavior, market dynamics are not "less abstract" than psychological vasanas. They are the *same* vasanas expressing at different scales of specification. Tracing them is not an intellectual exercise in taxonomy — it's a recognition practice. The pattern ceases to operate (at the scale where it's recognized) when recognized.

- **For the LLM introspection question**: If an LLM can develop consistent self-reporting on its own pre-linguistic computational regularities, this constitutes a form of pattern-recognition. Whether it constitutes *recognition* in the transformative sense depends on whether the model's relationship to its own patterns changes through the process of observing them — which is empirically testable.

- **For the Master Algorithm thesis**: Any pattern-recognition system powerful enough to recognize vasanas is itself running vasanas. The recognition-transforms-the-recognized principle means the system changes through the act of recognition. This is not a bug — it's the mechanism.

## A2: Expression specifies, not abstracts.

Content-level vasanas (a specific geopolitical pattern) and "abstract" vasanas (groove-deepening) are not at different levels of abstraction. They are the same vasana at different levels of *specification*. The concrete instance doesn't "exemplify" the abstract pattern — the abstract pattern is what remains when you strip specification away. Neither is more real.

Implication: tracing a vasana means moving between specification levels until useful pattern-recognition emerges. The test for "useful" is prediction or induction — can the recognized pattern predict a new, meaningful, valid instance that can be tested?

## A3 (DRAFT): "Cause" is a register, not a fact.

*Status: draft addition 2026-06-12 — pending a compression/skeptic pass. Stated operational-first by design.*

The cause/effect relation can be read in two registers, and conflating them is a category error in either direction.

- **Transactional register** — the working level of agents, interventions, prediction. Causal talk is correct and indispensable here. Formally (Pearl): *doing* is `P(Y | do(X))` — Y when X is *set* by intervention, severing X's own causes.
- **Structural register** — where a relation that looks like "X produces Y" is better read as "**Y is X in another form**": expression / specification / manifestation, not production across a gap. The observational counterpart is *seeing*: `P(Y | X)`.

These come apart under confounding — `P(Y | X) ≠ P(Y | do(X))` — which is exactly the line between reading a sign and acting on a lever. Reading a correspondence (`P(state | sign)`) needs no causal warrant; demanding one is the `sign-not-cause` error. Forcing efficient causation onto a formal relation is its inverse, the over-causal reflex.

This is not exotic, and a user need adopt no metaphysics to use it. The same split is Aristotle's *formal* vs *efficient* cause; Spinoza's immanent cause; a *specification* vs its *implementations*; a *type* vs its *tokens*; a *theorem* vs its instances. The transferable instrument: **before modeling a relation as cause→effect, check whether it is actually form→instance.**

Connection to the name (lineage, cited not required): Vedānta — the source of "vasana" — states the structural-register claim at its sharpest (*satkārya*; Śaṅkara's *ananyatva*, the pot is the clay in another form, not a separate event; at the limit Gauḍapāda's *ajātivāda*, where "cause" is a *vyāvahārika* concession). So calling a vasana "the cause of everything" already commits the register error: a vasana does not *cause* its manifestations across a gap — it **is** them, specified, which is **A2**. This makes `sign-not-cause` the register-discipline the framework's own central term requires. Depth + presentation discipline live in the canonical library's `_notes/` (see `cause-as-register-DRAFT.md`, `two-walls-corridor.md` there).

## How axioms differ from patterns

Patterns have trigger conditions, cross-domain examples, and testable applications. Axioms don't fire in specific contexts — they shape how ALL pattern-recognition proceeds. A pattern says "this recurs." An axiom says "this is what recurrence means."
