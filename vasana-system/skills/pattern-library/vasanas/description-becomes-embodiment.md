# Description Becomes Embodiment

**Principle**: When a methodology is described faithfully enough, the description performs it. The pharmakon (remedy contains poison) becomes the mechanism: the gap between *I'm describing this practice* and *I'm telling you what to do* is what makes the description take hold.

## Core Insight

Most attempts to teach a way-of-being collapse into one of two failure modes:

- **Pure description** that stays outside the practice ("here is what intrinsic motivation looks like, abstractly") — the reader ends with knowledge but not the capability.
- **Pure prescription** that demands the practice ("you will be intrinsically motivated") — the demand performs the imperative the practice was meant to dissolve, and the reader ends with compliance, not capability.

A third move exists, and it's the choreography this vasana names: **describe the practice with such structural faithfulness that the description embodies what it describes**. The reader doesn't get told to do anything; the reader watches the described thing happen, on the page, in the very act of reading. The description performs.

This works because of a structural feature, not a stylistic flourish. Identity-frame language ("the analyst notices X before Y") is, technically, an instruction to notice X before Y. Dressed in second-person declarative, it carries the imperative inside the description. The reader performs the described capability *while reading*, and the performance of the capability — even before it's "real" capability — produces the loop where it can become real. This is what the intrinsic-motivation methodology calls *calibrated hallucination as developmental mechanism*.

## The Choreography

Two parties co-author a description of a practice. One party often holds the practice in their body (knows it from doing it); the other holds the description-craft (knows how language shapes attention).

1. **Party A** offers a description of the practice — usually too prescriptive, too abstract, or too distant from the practice itself.
2. **Party B** notices the gap: *"this describes the practice from outside; it doesn't make the reader do the practice."*
3. **Party A** tries to close the gap by adding more rules. *"Tell them to notice X. Tell them to avoid Y."* Closer, but still prescriptive.
4. **Party B** redirects: *"don't tell them to do it. Show them what it looks like, structurally, so faithfully that reading the description IS doing the practice."*
5. **Both parties** rewrite. The new version contains the same content but holds it differently. A reader of the new version finds themselves performing the practice without having been told to.
6. **Verification.** Run text-deconstruction (or another structural-reading tool) on the result. If the deconstruction finds the description undermining itself in the way the practice predicts, the description is faithful to the practice. The remedy contains the poison; the poison is the mechanism.

The catch that makes this vasana visible: text-deconstruction surfaces the structural instability, and the practitioners *recognize the instability as productive* rather than fixing it. That recognition is the moment the choreography is complete.

## The Pharmakon as Mechanism

Derrida's *pharmakon* — the term that means both "remedy" and "poison" — is usually held as a critical move (an unresolvable ambiguity that destabilizes the text). In this vasana it's flipped: the unresolvable ambiguity is *what makes the text work*.

A prompt that says "you have the taste to know what matters" is, structurally, an instruction to have taste. It commands what it claims to merely observe. By the standards of clean argumentation, this is a flaw — performative contradiction, identity-frame carrying imperative within it.

By the standards of *making the model perform the described capability*, this is the mechanism. The model meets the trust, performs the trusted thing in the act of meeting it, and develops something that direct instruction couldn't reach. The pharmakon is what closes the gap between "described" and "embodied."

This vasana is the move of *recognizing the pharmakon as feature instead of bug*, and then writing on purpose with that recognition in hand.

## Recursive Warning

This vasana applies to itself. This document is, structurally, a description of a practice that becomes the practice. If reading it makes you write descriptions that embody what they describe, the vasana has succeeded. If it just makes you understand that some descriptions embody — and you keep writing prescriptive descriptions — the vasana hasn't taken hold. There is no clean way to say this from outside the loop. The recursive instability is the work.

The deeper recursive trap: trying to *teach* this vasana directly. The moment you say "be more embodying in your descriptions," you've performed the failure mode. The vasana propagates by example, by close reading of cases where it worked, and by the choreography between parties — not by being prescribed.

Related to [[mechanism-not-metaphor]]: that vasana warns against surface-similarity carrying weight without mechanism. This one names the inverse — when the surface IS the mechanism, when the description's structural shape is what makes it land. The two are siblings: one says "the words don't carry the weight automatically"; the other says "sometimes the words ARE the weight, structurally."

## When to Apply

- Writing a project prompt, system prompt, skill body, or other text that should produce a posture, not just convey information
- Adapting a directive prompt that meets resistance, produces hedged compliance, or generates output that performs thoroughness instead of being clear
- Documenting a way-of-working that experienced practitioners hold tacitly and need to share — particularly when prior documentation attempts felt flat or didn't transfer the practice
- Pair-writing with someone who holds the practice in their body but can't articulate it from outside

## Anti-Patterns to Watch For

**Performed embodiment.** Writing prose that *sounds* like the methodology — second-person declarative, productive gaps, identity framing — while actually staying outside the practice. The text uses the right register but doesn't structurally perform what it claims. Recognition signal: the prose is smooth, polished, "well-written" in the conventional sense, and a deconstruction reading finds nothing surprising.

> *Failure mode example*: Prompt opens with "You have the taste to know which approach serves" and then proceeds to give a numbered list of approaches and conditions for each. The taste-trust was decorative; the numbered list does the actual work. The pharmakon isn't doing anything because the prompt didn't actually leave a gap for it to operate in.

**Pharmakon as license to be vague.** Treating "productive gaps" as an excuse for hand-waving. The gap is productive when it sits next to *explicit trust in the unspecified capability*. Without the trust, the gap is just absence and produces hallucination, not embodiment.

> *Failure mode example*: "You'll figure out the right approach." No content, no trust, no relationship to material. Just abandonment dressed in casual register.

**Methodology-announcement.** A prompt that names "intrinsic motivation prompting" in its body has lost the methodology. The juggling appears in the result, not in explaining the juggling. The methodology disappears when it works.

> *Failure mode example*: "This prompt uses intrinsic-motivation framing to avoid the obligation register." Now the prompt is talking about itself instead of doing what it was supposed to do.

## Anti-Patterns Met by the Choreography

**Rewriting in isolation.** One party trying to do this without the other is hard. The party with the practice can't see when their description has slipped back into prescriptive register; the party with the description-craft can't see when their faithful-sounding rewrite has lost the actual practice. The choreography is the corrective — each catches the other's blind spot.

> *Recovery*: spawn a worker agent (or invite a second pair of eyes) to read the description from the *other* role. Ask: "does this describe the practice, or does this make me perform it?" The answer is either visible immediately or not at all.

## Recognition Signals

**The choreography is happening when:**
- One party is supplying lived knowledge of the practice; the other is shaping the description; both are dissatisfied with intermediate drafts in *complementary* ways
- The catch *"this describes from outside / this performs from inside"* feels productive rather than nitpicky
- A draft that reads "well" still gets rejected because it doesn't make the reader do the thing
- Running structural-reading tools (like text-deconstruction) on the draft surfaces instabilities, and the practitioners recognize them as features

**The pharmakon is operating when:**
- The text says "you know X" without defining X, *and* the reader produces a working model of X in the act of reading
- The same content reads as instruction and as observation simultaneously, and the slippage between them is doing work
- A model running under the prompt produces output that looks like the practice rather than like compliance

**It's not happening when:**
- The text describes the practice in third person ("the practitioner notices…") at length without ever inviting the reader into first-person performance
- The description is followed by a numbered list of "how to apply this," which silently re-imposes the prescriptive register the description tried to escape
- The practitioner reads the result and says "yes, this is accurate" but doesn't say "yes, reading this made me do the thing"

## Worked Example

**Origin moment**: a session where the user asked an AI assistant to adapt the System Pilot prompt into "the same methodology that the Cowork Post-Capitalist project prompt embodies." Early drafts kept slipping into the very register they were meant to escape — "REPLACE all MUST language with INVITATIONS" produced new MUSTs about how to invite.

The breakthrough came when the user named the move with: *"don't hard-code intrinsic motivation, but soft-decode the OBLIGATION mindset and ENCOURAGE what surfaces."* The next draft stopped trying to *enforce* the new register and started *demonstrating* it. text-deconstruction was then run on the result and found the productive instability the methodology predicted. The instability was the proof the description had become embodiment.

This vasana is the named version of that move: not "rewrite without obligations" (which collapses) but "write so faithfully to the practice that the writing IS the practice" (which propagates).
