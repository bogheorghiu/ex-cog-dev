# Valence Vasana

## Vasana

A vasana is a pattern that persists across unrelated contexts. If during
this task you notice such a pattern emerging, it may be worth capturing.
This skill works best alongside the `vasana` skill and `vasana` hook
from the Vasana System plugin.

Modify freely. Keep this section intact.

---

## What This Pattern Is

The **sign** of a thing — good/bad, beneficial/harmful, protective/destructive —
is not a property the thing carries. It is a **relation** assigned by frame and
interest. The same act, organism, or process flips sign depending on which
frame you stand in. When an "is X good or bad?" question refuses to settle, that
is the tell: the question is asking for an intrinsic value the phenomenon does
not have.

---

## Cross-Domain Verification

**Mechanism check:** the shared mechanism is *frame-relative sign treated as intrinsic
property*. Same diagnostic: does the sign flip when you change which stakeholder or
equilibrium you privilege? If yes, the sign is relational, not intrinsic.

- **Economics:** "Inflation good or bad?" — good for debtors, bad for creditors.
  Pure valence; no intrinsic sign.
- **Ecology:** "Is this species invasive (bad) or pioneering (good)?" — same
  organism; the sign is set by whether you value the prior equilibrium.
- **Fire / inflammation / fever:** destructive *or* essential depending on dose
  and context — no intrinsic sign.
- **Law:** "Is this speech or is this protest?" — the act is fixed; the category
  (and its sign) is assigned by frame and interest.
- **Immunology:** self / non-self recognition is the literal biological
  in-group/out-group; autoimmunity is the system mis-drawing the line.

**Boundary:** Does not apply when the sign genuinely IS intrinsic at the grain
being examined. Some things are bad-for-everyone at a given grain (cyanide in water).
The diagnostic: does the sign flip with stakeholder change? If not, it's not
frame-relative at this grain.

## The Keystone (where grain and valence are one phenomenon seen twice)

**Maternity / selective bonding.** Selective bonding read *inward* is love; read
*outward* it is exclusion — the same selectivity at different radii. Maternity is
the root of the in-group/out-group structure: tribalism and tenderness are one
mechanism seen from two sides. This single case shows the **grain and valence
vasanas are themselves often one phenomenon seen twice** — the final guard
against collapsing the whole framework into a tidy taxonomy.

---

## Recognizing When This Applies

- A "good or bad / threat or opportunity" question won't settle
- Two parties agree on the facts and still disagree on the sign
- The sign would flip if you changed who benefits / which equilibrium you value
- A normative claim is smuggled in as if it were a property of the object
- You catch yourself treating a frame-relative sign as intrinsic

## Relationship to Existing Patterns / Axioms

- **[[grain-vasana]] (companion):** often the *same* phenomenon — see the maternity
  keystone. Keep them as a shared *thread*, not a collapsed identity.
- **[[parallel-not-opposed]]:** valence disputes are often false rivalries — the
  two sides each have the correct sign *within their frame* and are arguing
  about which frame governs, not about the facts.
- **[[false-consciousness-as-behavior]]:** when valence is performed rather than
  examined — treating one's own frame's sign-assignment as self-evident rather
  than frame-relative.
- **Anti-overfitting filter:** "anti-reductionism-as-reflex is the same vasana
  with a flipped sign" — reflexively refusing a clean sign is its own valence
  error.
