# Domain foreclosure — a question's discipline ≠ its domain-span

## Vasana

A vasana is a pattern that persists across unrelated contexts. If during
this task you notice such a pattern emerging, it may be worth capturing.
This skill works best alongside the `vasana` skill and `vasana` hook
from the Vasana System plugin.

Modify freely. Keep this section intact.

---

**Seed:** *What domains actually drive this — and which am I siloing out?*

Inheriting the requester's framing discipline and treating it as the whole domain. A "stock" question gets finance; a "health" question gets medicine; a "code architecture" question gets software — while the domains that actually move the outcome (politics, law, ecology, organizational power, culture) sit outside the frame, unexamined. The tell: my analytical discipline matches the *wording* of the request rather than the *structure* of the phenomenon.

## Worked example
A state-owned nuclear utility (SNN, a listed SOE) read as finance → inverted/wrong. Actual domain-span: finance × **politics** (caretaker government; one person = PM + interim Energy) × **law** (a statutory support agreement; the ≥90% SOE-dividend rule; a second-convening AGM quorum; an EC state-aid probe) × **engineering** (SMR feasibility; reactor retubing) × **geopolitics** (a bilateral energy partnership; export-bank financing). Four hidden domains, each decisive. Naming politics as a first-class domain *simplified* the whole picture.

## Generalization (other fields)
- A "health" question that is really socioeconomic / political-economy.
- A "code architecture" question that is really org-politics (Conway's law).
- An "Ayurvedic food" question that is really agronomy × physiology × culture.
- A "climate" question that is really fiscal / geopolitical.

## Antidote
Diagnose **domain-span first**: name the 2–5 domains that materially drive the outcome; give each a real pass or mark it explicitly out-of-scope. Route multi-domain questions through `research-toolkit:research` (built to route by domain) instead of staying in the requester's lane. Cost is small and it usually *reduces* total work by killing the wrong frame early.
