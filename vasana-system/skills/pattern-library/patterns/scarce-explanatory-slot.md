**Seed question:** Does this explanation actually crowd the other one out — or am I assuming there's only one seat?

## What This Pattern Is

A single explanatory "seat" is silently assumed, so producing one adequate explanation is taken to *evict* all others: "If A explains it, B is redundant." But explanations stack across levels and aspects; the adequacy of one rarely entails the idleness of another. The classic product is **epiphenomenalism**: the mechanical account "fills the seat," so experience gets demoted to a passenger that rides along but does no work.

The hidden inference is **sufficient → exclusive**. "A is sufficient to explain the act" does *not* entail "B is absent or idle." The slot-scarcity assumption smuggles the exclusivity in.

**Origin:** Split off 2026-05-30 from [[parallel-not-opposed]], where it was one of three mechanisms manufacturing false rivalry. It earns standalone status because it fires *outside* false-rivalry too — wherever someone hunts for "the one real cause."

## Recognizing When This Applies

- "If X explains it, then Y adds nothing."
- A search for **the** single real cause, as if causes were rivals for one chair.
- Overdetermination treated as a paradox to be dissolved, rather than as a sign the one-slot assumption is wrong.
- Reductionist "nothing-but": the lower-level account is taken to vacate the higher-level one.

**Not this pattern:**
- Genuinely competing hypotheses *at the same level* — there the slot really is scarce and explanations do exclude each other ("bacterial vs viral cause" of one illness). Then: argue it.

## The Two Conflations That Power It

1. **Sufficient read as exclusive.** Enough-to-explain treated as nothing-else-operating.
2. **Levels read as competitors.** Neural firing and deciding are not two causes racing for one slot — they are one event described at two grains. Forcing them to compete invents the scarcity.

## Cross-Domain Verification

**Mechanism check (not metaphor):** the shared mechanism is *an unexamined assumption of a single explanatory slot, plus the sufficient→exclusive inference*. Same inference, checkable the same way, across:
- Philosophy of mind — epiphenomenalism (physical fills the slot → experience idle).
- Causation — overdetermination and the levels problem.
- Genetics — "the gene *for* X" vs environment (both operate).
- Social science — individual vs structural explanation (both real; neither vacates the other).
- ML interpretability — one salient feature "explains" a prediction vs genuinely distributed cause.

**Boundary:** does not apply where rival explanations share a level and are mutually exclusive. Discriminator: are the candidates at the *same* level and incompatible (one seat), or at *different* levels/aspects (no scarcity)?

## What Makes It Work / What Breaks It

- Works: ask "what makes me think there's only one seat?" then check the level of each explanation.
- Breaks: used to dodge real same-level competition ("they're just different levels, man") — the same abuse risk as parallel-not-opposed. The level-check is the guardrail.
