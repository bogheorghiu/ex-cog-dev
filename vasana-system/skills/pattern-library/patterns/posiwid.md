## What This Pattern Is

**POSIWID** — "the Purpose Of a System Is What It Does" (Stafford Beer):
judge a system by its behavior/output, not its stated intent. A recognized
principle with a named lineage, recorded here because it recurs across
*unrelated* contexts in exactly this library's sense — and because, before
naming it, it was already the unnamed lens behind several of this
project's own working principles.

**Origin:** Staged publicly as ex-cog-dev issue #88 (Jun 7, 2026; snapshot
in the excogitations archive), which ordered a *manual* library entry
because `record-pattern` was broken at the time — recorded 2026-06-12 via
`record-pattern`, which now works (the issue's blocker note is stale).

## Recognizing When This Applies

**Conditions:**
- Evaluating any system that *has* a stated purpose: an org, a tool, a
  methodology, a policy, a metric, a "debiasing" framework.
- Marketing/reality divergence — the stated purpose and the observable
  output disagree; POSIWID says the output is the purpose.
- Reflexively, on your own apparatus: did the debiasing pass introduce a
  bias? what does the security tool *do* (not promise)? (POSIWID composed
  with `self-application`.)

**Not this pattern:**
- Reading malice into every gap — POSIWID is symmetric and assumes no
  villain; "what it does" may diverge from intent through drift,
  incentives, or incompetence. It names the divergence, not a culprit.
- Systems too young to have behavior — POSIWID needs output to judge by;
  for designs-on-paper, it's a question to schedule, not a verdict.

## Cross-Domain Verification

**Mechanism check:** genuine shared structural principle — judge by
effects; behavior is the only observable a system can't misstate. Passed
in the staging issue and holds: nothing metaphorical transfers between
occurrences, the criterion itself does.

**Domains verified (already operating, unnamed, in this project):**

**Kin in this library:** `false-consciousness-as-behavior` — also

held distinct. `self-application` — POSIWID-on-itself is a worked instance.

**Lineage:** Stafford Beer, management cybernetics. Named so the principle
is checkable against prior art, not as borrowed authority.

## What Makes It Work

1. **Behavior is unforgeable** — every other purpose-signal (mission
   statement, docs, branding) is authored; output is what happened.
2. **Symmetry** — applied to the systems you like as much as the ones you
   don't, including your own; that's what keeps it a method rather than an
   accusation engine.
3. **Composability** — it slots into other tools as a step (cui-bono's
   tool-check, self-audit passes) rather than demanding its own ceremony.

## Testing This Pattern

Before relying on this:
1. **Baseline:** Evaluate a tool/org from its documentation and claims.
2. **With pattern:** Evaluate another from observed output first, claims second.
3. **Compare:** Where did the two verdicts diverge, and which held up?
4. **Pressure:** Does it work when rushed?
