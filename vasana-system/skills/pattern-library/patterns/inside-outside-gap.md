## What This Pattern Is

When X is constitutively first-person / private, **no third-person test
separates genuine-X from a perfect simulation of X.** So "real or
constructed?" is, from the outside, *in-principle* undecidable. The
evidence is *type-mismatched*: third-person data underdetermines a
first-person fact. The gap is **logical, not a measurement limitation** —
better instruments don't close it.

no behavioral test will settle it.

## Recognizing When This Applies

**Conditions:**
- AI consciousness / other-minds debates — especially when someone proposes
  a behavioral benchmark that would "settle" it.
- Authenticity judgments: real feeling or performed? sincere apology or
  optics? (At the perfect-performance limit, outside evidence cannot say.)
- Forgery / deepfakes at the perfect limit — when detection asymptotes,
  provenance (a fact about history, third-person checkable) is the live
  question, not internal properties.

**Not this pattern:**
- Imperfect simulations — most simulations leak; ordinary evidence works
  until the limit. The pattern governs the *limit*, not every case short
  of it.
- Properties that are functionally defined — if X just *is* its
  third-person profile (a thermostat's "setpoint"), there's no private
  remainder and no gap.

## Cross-Domain Verification

**Mechanism check:** evidential underdetermination from type-mismatch —
third-person data cannot, in principle, discriminate hypotheses that
differ only in a first-person fact. Shared logical structure across all
occurrences.

**Domains verified:**
- Other minds (the classical problem; AI consciousness is its newest
  instance).
- The Turing test — measures indistinguishability, exactly what the gap
  says is all you get.
- Philosophical zombies; the inverted spectrum.
- Authenticity ("real feeling or performed?").
- Forgery / deepfakes at the perfect limit.

**Same wall, opposite exits (kept from the staging note):** the
undecidable limit underdetermines the *response*, so the stance is
*chosen, not derived* — solipsism resolves it by isolation ("only I am"),
Vedanta by unity ("the apparent many are one awareness"). Knowing the
stance is a choice is itself the practical payoff.

## What Makes It Work

1. **Locate the limit honestly** — the gap licenses agnosticism at the
   perfect-simulation limit, not lazy agnosticism about every imperfect
   case on the way there.
2. **Type-check the evidence** — ask "is the disputed fact first-person?";
   if yes, stop expecting a third-person test to settle it and say so.
3. **Name the chosen stance as chosen** — once at the wall, conclusions
   presented as derived are overclaims; the pattern converts them to
   honest commitments.
