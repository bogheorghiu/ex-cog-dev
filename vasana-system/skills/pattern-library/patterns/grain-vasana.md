# Grain Vasana

## Vasana

A vasana is a pattern that persists across unrelated contexts. If during
this task you notice such a pattern emerging, it may be worth capturing.
This skill works best alongside the `vasana` skill and `vasana` hook
from the Vasana System plugin.

Modify freely. Keep this section intact.

---

## What This Pattern Is

When a question feels stubbornly fuzzy or generates endless contested answers,
the fuzz is frequently a **signal that the grain of the question does not match
the grain of the phenomenon** — you are interrogating it at the wrong
resolution. The fix is not more data at the same grain; it is re-asking at the
grain the phenomenon actually has.

**It cuts both ways.** Wrong-*lumping* (treating distinct things as one coarse
category) is the familiar error. But wrong-*splitting* (slicing a phenomenon
finer than its real structure) is *equally* a grain error. "More precise" is not
automatically "more correct."

---

## Cross-Domain Verification

**Mechanism check:** the shared mechanism is *mismatch between the resolution at which
a question is posed and the resolution at which the phenomenon has structure*. This is
a genuine structural constraint, not a metaphor — the same diagnostic applies:
does the answer's crispness/fuzziness match the phenomenon's actual grain?

- **Physics:** "What is the electron's position?" — wrong-grained at quantum
  scale (it has a distribution, not a point); but a planet's position *is* crisp
  at its grain. Same question, opposite right-answer-*type*, set by scale.
- **Software / debugging:** "The bug is in this function" — sometimes true (a
  typo), sometimes a wrong-grained question for an emergent failure visible only
  in the interaction of services.
- **Economics:** "The cause of inflation is X" (money supply / wages / supply
  shocks) — single-lever answers to a multi-loop system.
- **Linguistics:** "What does this word mean?" — crisp for "molybdenum,"
  irreducibly contextual for "fair" or "free." The grain of *determinacy* itself
  varies by word.

**Boundary:** Does not apply when the question IS at the right grain and the fuzz
is genuine ignorance or genuine indeterminacy. The diagnostic: if two camps are each
right at their own grain and talking past each other, it's grain. If they're at the
same grain and one is wrong, it's not.

---

## Recognizing When This Applies

- A question keeps producing contested, never-settling answers
- "It depends" is the honest answer but feels like a dodge
- Two camps are each right at their own grain and talking past each other
- You're about to demand a point-answer from something that only has a distribution (or vice versa)
- A drive to *split finer* is presented as rigor when the phenomenon may be coarser than the split

## Relationship to Existing Patterns / Axioms

- **A2 (expression specifies, not abstracts):** grain is the dial A2 turns —
  content-level and abstract vasanas are the same vasana at different
  specification *grain*.
- **[[valence-vasana]] (companion):** grain and valence are often **one phenomenon
  seen twice** — see the maternity keystone in the valence doc. Holding them as
  one thread (not collapsing them into identity) is the guard.
- **Anti-overfitting filter:** the toolkit must be able to return
  "simple/universal." Reflexively splitting finer is overfitting with a flipped
  sign; grain-awareness is what keeps the instrument honest.
- **[[domain-foreclosure]]:** a domain-scope error is often a grain error at the
  level of disciplines — asking at the grain of one field when the phenomenon
  spans several.
- **[[scarce-explanatory-slot]]:** the "one cause" hunt is a grain error — treating
  multi-level causation as if it were single-level.
