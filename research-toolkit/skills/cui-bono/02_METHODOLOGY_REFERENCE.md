## 1. Western Intelligence Tradecraft

### Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH)
**Source:** Richards Heuer, *Psychology of Intelligence Analysis* (1999); CIA *Tradecraft Primer* (2009)

**Core principle:** Work ACROSS the evidence matrix (one item against all hypotheses) rather than DOWN (one hypothesis against all evidence). This prevents confirmation bias.

**Key insight:** Missing evidence (what SHOULD be observable if hypothesis is true) is often more diagnostic than present evidence. Evidence consistent with all hypotheses has zero diagnostic value.

### UK PHIA Probability Yardstick
**Source:** UK Professional Head of Intelligence Assessment

| Term | Probability Range |
|------|-------------------|
| Remote chance | ≤5% |
| Highly unlikely | 10-20% |
| Unlikely | 25-35% |
| Realistic possibility | 40-<50% |
| Likely | 55-75% |
| Highly likely | 80-90% |
| Almost certain | ≥95% |

**Note:** Gaps between ranges reduce ambiguity. No term conveys absolute certainty.

### Admiralty System (NATO STANAG 2511)
**Source:** British Naval Intelligence, WWII; standardized across NATO

**Source Reliability:**
- A: Completely reliable
- B: Usually reliable
- C: Fairly reliable
- D: Not usually reliable
- E: Unreliable
- F: Cannot be judged

**Information Credibility:**
- 1: Confirmed by independent sources
- 2: Probably true
- 3: Possibly true
- 4: Doubtful
- 5: Improbable
- 6: Cannot be judged

**Critical:** These are INDEPENDENT dimensions. A-rated source can deliver 4-rated information.

### Israeli Devil's Advocacy
**Source:** Post-Yom Kippur War reforms; documented failures in October 7, 2023

**Lesson:** Institutionalized contrarian roles (Review Department) are insufficient without broader analytical culture. Two September 2023 assessments warned of Hamas attack changes; senior officials ignored both.

## 2. Russian Analytical Traditions

### Reflexive Control (рефлексивное управление)
**Source:** Vladimir Lefebvre (theory); Col. S.A. Komov (operationalization)

**Definition:** Substitute motivation factors in adversary's decision-making to encourage "voluntarily" making predetermined decision.

**Components:**
- Maskirovka (strategic deception)
- Disinformation ("polluted information")
- Provocation (triggering events)
- Kompromat (compromising materials)

**Application:** When investigating Russian actors, recognize what information may be deliberately planted.

### Russian Independent Journalism Standards
**Sources:** Meduza Code of Conduct; The Insider/Christo Grozev methodology; Proekt Media

**Key techniques:**
- Multiple sources for EVERY significant claim
- No revision of remarks after interviews (contrary to Russian PR practice)
- Black market data acquisition (cell phone metadata, flight records)
- Passport number pattern analysis for security service identification
- Cross-referencing travel records with incidents
- **Standard:** Evidence must be publicly verifiable—creating refutable documentation that cannot be denied through disinformation

**Why superior:** Russian independent journalists face existential threat from state; survival depends on being irrefutably correct.

## 3. Chinese Analytical Frameworks

### Comprehensive National Power (综合国力)
**Source:** Party Charter (1992); CASS model

**Eight assessment dimensions:**
1. Economic resources
2. Human capital
3. Natural resources
4. Capital resources
5. Knowledge/technology
6. Government resources
7. Military resources
8. International resources

**Application:** Holistic entity assessment reveals how weakness in one dimension may be compensated by strength in others.

### Shi (势) - Strategic Configuration
**Source:** Sun Tzu; contemporary Chinese strategic thought

**Definition:** Strategic momentum, positioning, potential born of disposition

**Application:** Assess market flow and positioning; identify structural advantages/vulnerabilities; evaluate timing and momentum factors.

### Contradiction Analysis (矛盾分析)
**Source:** Mao Zedong, "On Contradiction" (1937)

**Method:**
1. Identify all contradictions
2. Distinguish principal from secondary (principal's resolution is decisive)
3. Identify principal aspect (which side is dominant)
4. Track transformation (principal/secondary can switch)

### Chinese Investigative Journalism
**Source:** Caixin methodology (GIJN documentation)

**Sourcing hierarchy:**
1. Official government databases (National Enterprise Credit Information System)
2. Financial data terminals (Wind)
3. State media for *official viewpoint* (not necessarily factual)
4. International organization data

**Critical insight:** "If it's in Wuhan, you typically can't trust Wuhan media"—local media face punishment for reporting on local government. Seek higher-level sources.

### Chinese Framing of Western Power
**Sources:** Chinese Marxist academia; Monthly Review (Cheng Enfu)

**Five characteristics of neoimperialism:**
1. New monopoly of production
2. Monopoly of financial capital
3. Monopoly of USD and intellectual property
4. International oligarchic alliance monopoly
5. Globalized contradictions of capitalism

**Application:** Understanding how Chinese analysts frame Western corporate power reveals assumptions invisible within Western frameworks.

## 4. Global South Investigative Methods

### Story-Based Inquiry (ARIJ Method)
**Source:** Arab Reporters for Investigative Journalism; UNESCO manual (2009)

**Principles:**
1. Story is only a hypothesis until verified
2. Decompose hypothesis: Subject + Verb + Object (each needs independent verification)
3. Build chronological timeline from open sources first
4. Regular hypothesis testing after each new evidence item
5. Be willing to abandon hypotheses that fail

### Bulletproofing (amaBhungane)
**Source:** South African Centre for Investigative Journalism; #GuptaLeaks methodology

**Standards:**
1. Data supports story (never vice versa)
2. Prosecutorial cross-examination of every major claim
3. Multiple verification paths (documentary + testimonial + physical)
4. Legal challenge anticipation

**Process:** "Cross-examination" by prosecutorial editor; academic peer review partnership; convergence of journalism, data, and law.

### RTI Methodology (India)
**Source:** Shyamlal Yadav (Indian Express, ICIJ)

**Method:** File Right to Information requests at multiple governmental levels simultaneously (central, state, district, block); pursue appeals; transform raw information into narrative.

### Latin American Investigative Journalism
**Sources:** El Faro (El Salvador), CIPER Chile

**El Faro "penetration technique":** "We were able to penetrate these criminal structures and collect the stories they told about themselves."

**CIPER Chile:** Pioneered use of transparency legislation during investigations—model replicated across Latin America.

## 5. Critical/Alternative Frameworks

### Dependency Theory (ECLAC/Prebisch)
**Source:** Raúl Prebisch; UN Economic Commission for Latin America

**Core concept:** Centre-periphery analysis—asymmetric dynamics between developed (centre) and developing (periphery) nations through unequal technological diffusion and terms of trade.

**Application:** Who benefits from extraction, who bears costs? Trace center-periphery dynamics in corporate relationships.

### Coloniality of Power (Quijano)
**Source:** Aníbal Quijano, Peruvian sociologist

**Four domains of colonial matrix:**
1. Control of economy (land, labor, resources)
2. Control of authority (institutions, military)
3. Control of gender and sexuality
4. Control of subjectivity and knowledge

**Application:** Does corporate presence perpetuate colonial patterns in each domain?

### Epistemologies of the South (Santos)
**Source:** Boaventura de Sousa Santos, University of Coimbra

**Four tools:**
1. **Sociology of Absences:** What has been made invisible by dominant knowledge?
2. **Sociology of Emergences:** What alternatives exist?
3. **Ecology of Knowledges:** Value diverse knowledge forms
4. **Intercultural Translation:** Dialogue between knowledge systems

### Feminist Standpoint Epistemology
**Sources:** Sandra Harding (strong objectivity); Patricia Hill Collins (outsider within); Kimberlé Crenshaw (intersectionality)

**Strong objectivity:** Start research from lives of the marginalized, not dominant group. The higher the level of oppression, the less distorted the account.

**Intersectional method:** When seeing race problem, ask "Where is the gender dimension?" When seeing gender, ask "Where is the class dimension?"

### Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk)
**Source:** Mi'kmaw Elder Albert Marshall

**Definition:** "Learning to see from one eye with the strengths of Indigenous knowledges... and from the other eye with the strengths of Western knowledges... and learning to use both these eyes together."

**Warning:** "Can all too easily slip into lazy, tokenistic approach"—genuine co-learning requires mutual vulnerability, not technique extraction.

### Gramscian Hegemony Analysis
**Source:** Antonio Gramsci, *Prison Notebooks*

**Method:**
1. Identify relations of forces at three levels (economic, political, coercive)
2. Map balance of force vs. consent
3. Identify historical bloc (alliance of class forces)
4. Identify organic intellectuals (who serves vs. challenges corporate interests)
5. Analyze "common sense" (what appears obvious about corporate arrangements; how was this constructed?)

### Liberation Theology See-Judge-Act
**Source:** Clodovis Boff; Latin American liberation theology

**Three mediations:**
1. **SEE:** Immerse in lived reality of the oppressed using social science tools critically
2. **JUDGE:** Interpret through ethical lens—whose perspective is prioritized? (preferential option for the poor)
3. **ACT:** Determine concrete action validated by effects on the marginalized

## 6. OSINT Techniques

### Bellingcat Verification
**Source:** Bellingcat methodology guides

**Geolocation:**
- Landmark identification against satellite imagery
- Multi-platform cross-referencing (Google Earth, Yandex Maps)
- Custom OpenStreetMap Search Tool
- GeoHints database (regional traffic lights, utility poles, bollards)

**Chronolocation:**
- Shadow analysis using SunCalc
- Shadow Finder Tool (maps all Earth points where given shadow could occur)
- Visual temporal markers (storefronts, seasonal foliage in Street View historical imagery)

**Evidence chain:** Transparent provenance; reference image verification; multi-source triangulation.

### ICIJ Radical Sharing
**Source:** Panama Papers methodology

**Technical:** Apache Solr + Tika for indexing; Neo4j graph database; Linkurious visualization

**Philosophy:** Journalists share discoveries in real-time with partners worldwide; cross-referencing at entity level; databases "talk to each other."

### Syrian Archive / Forensic Architecture
**Sources:** Syrian Archive (Duke EDRM model); Forensic Architecture

**Syrian Archive workflow:** Database of credible sources → standardized metadata → automated daily scraping → hash assignment + timestamping → "reasonable grounds to suspect" threshold for tags

**Forensic Architecture "counter forensics":** Architecture as sensor (analyzing material evidence), agent (constructing new forums), synthesizer (combining evidence forms). Techniques: 3D reconstruction, "ear witnessing" (reconstructing from acoustic memories), bomb cloud analysis.

## 7. Key Epistemological Tensions (Do Not Resolve Artificially)

| Tension | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Universal dignity vs. irreducible difference | Liberation theology's universalism vs. postcolonial emphasis on cultural specificity |
| Class vs. identity axes | Gramscian/Frankfurt class primacy vs. intersectional multiple irreducible axes |
| Research vs. action | Some frameworks privilege analysis; participatory action demands immediate intervention |
| Who can use these methods | Some traditions (Kaupapa Māori) were developed by/for specific communities—appropriation without accountability violates core principles |
| Objectivity claims | Western methods claim objectivity while embedding assumptions; critical methods acknowledge positioning while risking relativism |

## 8. Source Quality by Tradition

| Tradition | What Counts as Strong Evidence | Limitations |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Western OSINT | Documentary, verifiable, attributable, transparent provenance | Privileges literate/documented over oral/experiential |
| Russian Independent | Verifiable data (including acquired), methodology transparent, partner verified | Access constraints, personal risk |
| Chinese State | Comprehensive multi-domain, state media for official position | Ideological overlay, access limitations |
| Global South Journalism | Legally bulletproof, hypothesis-verified, cross-border corroborated | Resource constraints, safety risks |
| Indigenous Methods | Oral tradition, community testimony, relational knowing, elder verified | May lack documentary trail |
| Feminist Standpoint | Marginalized experience, embodied knowledge, affected party testimony | May conflict with institutional sources |
| Liberation Theology | Lived experience of oppressed, collective discernment | Explicitly normative |

## Quick Application Guide

**When investigating Western corporate power:**
- Apply Chinese CNP assessment and contradiction analysis
- Use dependency theory to trace center-periphery dynamics
- Apply Santos' sociology of absences (what alternatives suppressed?)
- Apply Gramscian hegemony analysis (whose "common sense"?)

**When investigating Chinese/Russian entities:**
- Apply same standards used for Western entities (symmetric)
- Use Western OSINT techniques with non-Western source awareness
- Check for reflexive control / planted information
- Apply indigenous/community standpoint (what do affected know?)

**When investigating extractive industries:**
- Apply coloniality analysis across all four domains
- Use feminist standpoint (what do workers/communities know?)
- Trace supply chain with dependency theory
- Check indigenous land rights with OCAP principles

**When claims seem obvious:**
- Apply Key Assumptions Check
- Use contradiction analysis (what tensions exist?)
- Apply sociology of absences (what's been made invisible?)
- Seek disconfirming evidence through ARIJ method
