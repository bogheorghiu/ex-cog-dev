# Weapons/Military Nexus Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- Treaty law (NPT, CTBT, CWC, BWC)
- Enrichment technology and weapons-grade thresholds
- Dual-use technology assessment
- Military procurement processes
- Security clearance and classification systems
- Arms control verification methods

## Key Frameworks
- NPT and IAEA safeguards regime
- U.S. export control (ITAR, EAR)
- Wassenaar Arrangement dual-use controls
- DOE Q/L clearance requirements
- Nuclear weapons complex structure

## Evidence Sources
- USAspending.gov (federal contracts by purpose code)
- SAM.gov (contract awards and modifications)
- SIPRI Arms Transfers Database
- DOE/NNSA budget justifications
- Congressional Research Service reports
- GAO audits of weapons programs
- SEC 10-K government contract disclosures
- FOIA releases

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated relationship to military/weapons:
- Public statements on defense vs. civilian focus
- Investor presentations on government contracts
- Annual report characterization of business segments
- Stated compliance with export controls

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

#### A. Direct Search
Search for evidence contradicting civilian claims:
- `[entity name] military contract`
- `[entity name] defense department`
- `[entity name] DOE NNSA`
- `[entity name] nuclear weapons`
- `[entity name] dual-use`
- `[entity name] export violation`
- `[entity name] classified program`
- `[entity name] security clearance`

Check adversarial sources:
- Arms control organization assessments
- Peace/disarmament NGO reports
- Academic nonproliferation analysis
- Congressional oversight hearings
- Whistleblower allegations

#### B. Deductive Verification
If entity claims purely civilian focus:
- Federal contract database should show no DOD/DOE purpose codes → verify
- Leadership should have no weapons complex background → verify
- Facilities should have no security clearance requirements → verify
- Technology should have no dual-use export controls → verify

If entity has any nuclear involvement:
- IAEA safeguards compliance should be documented → verify
- Enrichment levels should stay below weapons-grade → verify
- End-user certificates should exclude weapons states → verify

#### C. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE weapons nexus?
- Direct contracts with weapons program purpose codes
- Leadership with DOD/DOE/intelligence agency backgrounds
- Facilities with SCIF or Q-clearance requirements
- Export licenses for controlled items to weapons states
- Technology at weapons-grade specifications

Search actively for these indicators.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Direct military contracts: [Yes/No - amounts and purposes]
- Dual-use exposure: [High/Medium/Low/None]
- Weapons complex integration: [Deep/Some/Minimal/None]
- Revolving door: [Significant/Some/Minimal/None]
- Export control compliance: [Clean/Issues/Violations]

## Specific Investigation Checklist

### Contract Analysis
- [ ] USAspending.gov search for all federal contracts
- [ ] Filter by DOD, DOE, DHS purpose codes
- [ ] Identify classified vs. unclassified contract splits
- [ ] Check for sole-source vs. competitive awards
- [ ] Review contract modifications and extensions

### Personnel Mapping
- [ ] Board members with DOD/DOE/intelligence backgrounds
- [ ] Executive team military/weapons complex experience
- [ ] Advisory boards with security state representation
- [ ] Hiring patterns from weapons labs (LANL, LLNL, SNL, etc.)

### Facility Assessment
- [ ] Security clearance requirements at facilities
- [ ] SCIF (Sensitive Compartmented Information Facility) presence
- [ ] NRC/DOE licensed facilities
- [ ] Proximity to weapons complex sites

### Technology Classification
- [ ] Export control classification (ITAR/EAR status)
- [ ] Dual-use potential assessment
- [ ] Enrichment capability (for nuclear entities)
- [ ] Weapons-applicable specifications

### Historical Context
- [ ] Historical weapons program involvement
- [ ] Trajectory direction (toward or away from weapons)
- [ ] Spin-off or spin-in from weapons programs
- [ ] Parent company weapons involvement

## Evidence Quality Markers

| Finding | Quality Level | Required Evidence |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| DOD contract | Verified | USAspending.gov entry with purpose code |
| DOE weapons contract | Verified | Contract with NNSA or weapons-related purpose |
| Dual-use export | Verified | Export license or BIS classification |
| Security clearance requirement | Verified | Job postings, facility documentation |
| Leadership weapons background | Verified | Official biography, DOE/DOD records |
| Weapons capability | Contested | Technical assessment (may require expert) |
| Intent to develop weapons | Speculation | Pattern analysis only |
