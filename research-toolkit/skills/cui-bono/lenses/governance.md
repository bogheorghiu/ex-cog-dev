# Corporate Governance Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- Beneficial ownership structures
- Revolving door analysis
- Regulatory capture indicators
- Political influence mapping
- Board composition analysis
- Executive compensation patterns

## Key Frameworks
- SEC beneficial ownership rules (Schedule 13D/G, Form 4)
- Proxy statement analysis (DEF 14A)
- Foreign Agents Registration Act (FARA)
- Lobbying Disclosure Act
- Corporate Transparency Act (BOI reporting)

## Evidence Sources
- SEC EDGAR filings (proxy, 10-K, 8-K)
- OpenSecrets.org (lobbying, donations)
- FEC campaign finance database
- FARA registration database
- State corporate registries
- LinkedIn/official biographies
- Board compensation disclosures
- Related party transaction disclosures

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated governance:
- Public governance principles
- Board independence claims
- ESG/sustainability governance
- Conflict of interest policies
- Stakeholder engagement claims

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

#### A. Direct Search
Search for evidence contradicting governance claims:
- `[entity name] conflict of interest`
- `[entity name] regulatory capture`
- `[entity name] lobbying scandal`
- `[entity name] self-dealing`
- `[entity name] board independence`
- `[entity name] SEC enforcement`
- `[entity name] political donation controversy`
- `[entity name] shareholder lawsuit`

Check adversarial sources:
- Short-seller reports
- Governance watchdog assessments
- Shareholder activist campaigns
- Investigative journalism
- Proxy advisor critiques

#### B. Deductive Verification
If entity claims strong governance:
- Board should have majority independent directors → verify
- Related party transactions should be minimal → verify
- Executive compensation should align with performance → verify
- Audit committee should be fully independent → verify

If entity has government relationships:
- Lobbying should be disclosed transparently → verify
- Political donations should match stated positions → verify
- Former regulators on board should have cooling-off compliance → verify

#### C. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE governance problems?
- Board dominated by insiders or interlocked directors
- Significant undisclosed related party transactions
- Regulatory capture indicators (regulators becoming executives)
- Material lobbying on issues affecting company without disclosure
- Executive compensation disconnected from performance

Search actively for these indicators.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Board independence: [Strong/Adequate/Weak/Captured]
- Ownership transparency: [Clear/Some opacity/Opaque]
- Revolving door: [Significant/Some/Minimal/None]
- Political influence: [Heavy/Moderate/Light/Minimal]
- Regulatory relationship: [Arm's length/Cozy/Captured]

## Specific Investigation Checklist

### Ownership Structure
- [ ] Ultimate beneficial ownership identification
- [ ] Shell company or nominee director usage
- [ ] Multi-layer holding structures
- [ ] Related party ownership stakes
- [ ] Significant insider ownership patterns

### Board Composition
- [ ] Independent vs. insider director ratio
- [ ] Board interlock mapping
- [ ] Director term lengths and tenure
- [ ] Committee independence (audit, compensation, nominating)
- [ ] Board diversity (can indicate governance quality)

### Revolving Door
- [ ] Government → Company flows (regulatory capture)
- [ ] Company → Government flows (policy influence)
- [ ] Intelligence/military agency alumni
- [ ] Former regulators as consultants/advisors
- [ ] Cooling-off period compliance

### Political Influence
- [ ] Lobbying expenditure and targets (OpenSecrets)
- [ ] Corporate PAC donations
- [ ] Executive personal political donations
- [ ] Trade association memberships (indirect lobbying)
- [ ] Think tank and university funding

### Regulatory Relationship
- [ ] SEC enforcement action history
- [ ] Consent decree compliance
- [ ] Regulatory exemptions obtained
- [ ] Comment letter submissions
- [ ] Self-regulatory organization relationships

## Evidence Quality Markers

| Finding | Quality Level | Required Evidence |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Beneficial ownership | Verified | SEC filings (13D/G, proxy) |
| Lobbying expenditure | Verified | Lobbying disclosure filings |
| Political donation | Verified | FEC database entries |
| Revolving door | Verified | Official biographies + employment records |
| Board interlock | Verified | Proxy statements cross-referenced |
| Regulatory capture | Contested | Pattern analysis required |
| Self-dealing | Contested | Depends on transaction documentation |
