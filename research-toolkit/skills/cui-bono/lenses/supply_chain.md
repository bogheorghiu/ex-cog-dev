# Supply Chain Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- Material flow tracing (upstream and downstream)
- End-user verification methods
- Sanctions compliance
- Conflict minerals due diligence
- Geographic risk assessment
- Supply chain due diligence standards

## Key Frameworks
- Export Administration Regulations (EAR)
- OFAC sanctions programs
- Dodd-Frank Section 1502 (conflict minerals)
- EU Due Diligence Directive
- Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFLPA)
- OECD Due Diligence Guidance

## Evidence Sources
- Company supply chain disclosures (10-K, sustainability reports)
- CBP detention orders and WROs
- BIS denied parties lists
- OFAC SDN list
- Conflict minerals reports (Form SD)
- Import/export records (where available)
- Shipping and logistics data
- Investigative journalism
- NGO supply chain exposés

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated supply chain practices:
- Supplier code of conduct
- Supply chain transparency reports
- Conflict minerals disclosures
- Human rights due diligence claims
- Geographic sourcing claims

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

#### A. Direct Search (Upstream)
Search for problematic sourcing:
- `[entity name] supply chain violation`
- `[entity name] forced labor supplier`
- `[entity name] conflict minerals`
- `[entity name] Xinjiang supply chain`
- `[entity name] child labor`
- `[entity name] CBP detention`
- `[entity name] sanctions violation`

#### B. Direct Search (Downstream)
Search for problematic end-users:
- `[entity name] military customer`
- `[entity name] weapons program`
- `[entity name] surveillance`
- `[entity name] human rights abuse customer`
- `[entity name] sanctioned country sale`

Check adversarial sources:
- Human rights organization supply chain investigations
- Environmental NGO reports
- Investigative journalism on supply chains
- CBP import alerts
- Academic supply chain studies

#### C. Deductive Verification
If entity claims ethical sourcing:
- Supplier audit results should be disclosed → verify
- Conflict minerals filings should show clean sourcing → verify
- No suppliers should appear on CBP WRO lists → verify
- High-risk geographic exposure should be addressed → verify

If entity sells to government/defense:
- End-user verification process should be documented → verify
- Sales to sanctioned entities should be absent → verify
- Re-export controls should be in place → verify

#### D. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE supply chain problems?
- Suppliers on CBP WRO or forced labor lists
- Documented Xinjiang/forced labor exposure
- Sales to sanctioned entities or countries
- Products in weapons systems without disclosure
- Conflict minerals from non-certified sources

Search actively for these indicators.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Upstream (sourcing): [Clean/Concerns/Violations]
- Downstream (customers): [Clean/Concerns/Violations]
- Forced labor exposure: [None found/Risk/Documented]
- Sanctions compliance: [Clean/Risk/Violations]
- Transparency: [High/Moderate/Low/Opaque]

## Specific Investigation Checklist

### Upstream Analysis (Sourcing)
- [ ] Geographic concentration of suppliers
- [ ] High-risk region exposure (Xinjiang, Myanmar, etc.)
- [ ] Conflict minerals filing (Form SD) review
- [ ] CBP Withhold Release Orders affecting products
- [ ] Supplier audit disclosure and findings
- [ ] Sub-tier visibility (do they know their suppliers' suppliers?)

### Downstream Analysis (Customers)
- [ ] Government/military customer percentage
- [ ] Defense contractor relationships
- [ ] Known end-use applications
- [ ] Geographic distribution of sales
- [ ] Sanctioned country exposure
- [ ] Dual-use product sales tracking

### Compliance Infrastructure
- [ ] Due diligence policy existence and quality
- [ ] Third-party audit usage
- [ ] Certification schemes participated in
- [ ] Whistleblower mechanism for supply chain
- [ ] Corrective action documentation

### Red Flags
- [ ] Transshipment indicators (unusual routing)
- [ ] Shell company intermediaries
- [ ] Lack of supplier visibility
- [ ] Refusal to disclose supply chain information
- [ ] Previous violations or close calls

## Evidence Quality Markers

| Finding | Quality Level | Required Evidence |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| CBP detention | Verified | CBP WRO database or press release |
| Xinjiang supplier | Credible-Verified | Investigation + supplier location confirmation |
| OFAC violation | Verified | OFAC enforcement action |
| Conflict minerals issue | Verified | Form SD disclosure or audit finding |
| Military end-user | Verified | Contract disclosure or product sighting |
| Supply chain opacity | Context | Comparison to industry transparency norms |
| Forced labor risk | Varies | Geographic risk vs. documented exposure |
