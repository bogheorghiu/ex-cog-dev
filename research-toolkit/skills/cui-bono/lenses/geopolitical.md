# Geopolitical Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- Power structure mapping across multiple poles
- Imperial alignment analysis
- Dependency theory (center-periphery)
- Multi-vector positioning assessment
- Strategic infrastructure identification
- Weaponized interdependence analysis

## Key Frameworks
- Realist IR theory (power balancing)
- Dependency/world-systems theory (center-periphery)
- Weaponized interdependence (network chokepoints)
- Chinese Comprehensive National Power assessment
- Multi-polar analysis (symmetric across poles)

## Evidence Sources
- Foreign policy journals and analysis
- Trade and investment flow data
- Treaty participation records
- Diplomatic communications (when available)
- SIPRI and strategic studies institutes
- Regional expertise sources
- Critical infrastructure designations
- Ownership and control mapping

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated geopolitical position:
- Public statements on international relations
- Stated neutrality or alignment
- Market positioning across regions
- Government relationships disclosed
- Strategic partnership announcements

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

**CRITICAL: Apply analysis SYMMETRICALLY across power poles**

#### A. Western Structures
If entity connected to Western structures, examine:
- Dollar hegemony benefits/dependencies
- NATO/Five Eyes integration
- IMF/World Bank relationship patterns
- IP extraction via TRIPS/trade agreements
- Military base proximity and contracts
- Dollar-denominated financing dependence

#### B. Chinese Structures
If entity connected to Chinese structures, examine:
- BRI debt relationship patterns
- Technology transfer dynamics
- Xinjiang supply chain exposure
- South China Sea positioning
- State-owned enterprise integration
- CCP party committee presence

#### C. Russian Structures
If entity connected to Russian structures, examine:
- Energy dependency leverage
- Wagner/security contractor relationships
- Sanctions evasion facilitation
- Information warfare infrastructure
- Post-Soviet sphere influence patterns
- Oligarch ownership connections

#### D. Regional Powers
Examine connections to regional powers:
- Saudi/Gulf state financial relationships
- Iranian sanctions exposure
- Turkish sphere of influence
- Indian strategic positioning
- Brazilian/BRICS alignment
- Israeli technology/security ties

#### E. Deductive Verification
If entity claims neutrality/multi-vector positioning:
- Revenue should be geographically diversified → verify
- No single power's infrastructure should be critical → verify
- Multiple currency financing should be available → verify
- Political relationships should be balanced → verify

#### F. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE imperial alignment?
- Overwhelming revenue dependence on single power bloc
- Critical infrastructure designation by specific power
- Leadership with deep ties to single power's establishment
- Participation in sanctions enforcement or evasion
- Strategic chokepoint control serving specific power

Search actively across ALL poles.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Primary alignment: [Western/Chinese/Russian/Multi-vector/Regional]
- Dependency pattern: [Dependent/Interdependent/Autonomous]
- Strategic significance: [Critical infrastructure/Strategic/Standard/Marginal]
- Power served: [Which structures does entity strengthen?]
- Documented harms: [What harms are associated with those structures?]

## Multi-Polar Analysis Requirements

**The question is NOT "which pole is worse"**

The question IS:
1. What power structures does this entity serve/strengthen?
2. What are the DOCUMENTED harms of those structures?
3. Does investment in this entity support those harms?
4. Are there alternatives that serve different/less harmful structures?

**Apply same standards to all poles:**
- Western harms: Military interventions, economic coercion, IP extraction, dollar weaponization
- Chinese harms: Xinjiang, debt diplomacy, technology coercion, South China Sea
- Russian harms: Military aggression, energy weaponization, information warfare, autocracy export
- Regional harms: Document specifically per context
