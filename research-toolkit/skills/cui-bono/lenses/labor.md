# Labor Rights Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- ILO Core Conventions
- Occupational safety standards (OSHA equivalents)
- Collective bargaining and union law
- Forced labor and trafficking indicators
- Workers' compensation systems
- Industry-specific labor standards

## Key Frameworks
- ILO Core Conventions (8 fundamental)
- OSHA standards (U.S.) and equivalents
- NLRA and collective bargaining rights
- Fair Labor Standards Act
- Supply chain due diligence standards

## Evidence Sources
- OSHA inspection and violation database
- NLRB unfair labor practice cases
- ILO supervisory body findings
- Court records (labor lawsuits, settlements)
- Union reports and press releases
- Workers' compensation claims data
- Investigative journalism on labor conditions
- Worker testimony (Glassdoor, direct)
- State labor agency enforcement

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated labor practices:
- Sustainability reports on worker welfare
- Supplier codes of conduct
- Union recognition or neutrality policies
- Safety commitment statements
- Wage and benefit claims

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

#### A. Direct Search
Search for evidence contradicting labor claims:
- `[entity name] OSHA violation`
- `[entity name] labor lawsuit`
- `[entity name] union busting`
- `[entity name] wage theft`
- `[entity name] worker injury`
- `[entity name] unsafe conditions`
- `[entity name] forced labor`
- `[entity name] child labor supply chain`

Check adversarial sources:
- Union organizing reports
- Labor NGO assessments
- Worker rights organizations
- Investigative journalism
- NLRB case records
- Glassdoor reviews (pattern analysis)

#### B. Deductive Verification
If entity claims strong labor practices:
- OSHA violation rate should be minimal → verify
- NLRB complaints should be rare → verify
- Workers' compensation claims should be below industry average → verify
- Employee retention should be stable → verify

If entity has supply chain exposure:
- Supplier audit reports should be disclosed → verify
- Third-party certification should be current → verify
- Supply chain complaints should be addressed → verify

#### C. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE labor rights violations?
- OSHA citations above industry average
- NLRB unfair labor practice findings
- Wage theft lawsuits or settlements
- Documented forced labor exposure
- Worker deaths or serious injuries above baseline
- Union suppression evidence

Search actively for these indicators.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Safety record: [Good/Mixed/Poor]
- Union relations: [Positive/Neutral/Hostile/Suppressive]
- Wage practices: [Fair/Issues/Violations]
- Supply chain exposure: [Clean/Concerns/Violations]
- Worker voice: [Heard/Suppressed/Unknown]

## Specific Investigation Checklist

### Safety Record
- [ ] OSHA inspection history and violations
- [ ] Violation severity (willful, serious, repeat)
- [ ] Fatality and injury rates vs. industry
- [ ] Workers' compensation claim patterns
- [ ] Safety certification status

### Union Relations
- [ ] Union recognition status
- [ ] Collective bargaining agreement existence
- [ ] NLRB unfair labor practice cases
- [ ] Strike history and management response
- [ ] Anti-union consultant employment

### Wage and Hour
- [ ] DOL wage and hour investigations
- [ ] Class action lawsuits (wage theft, misclassification)
- [ ] Settlements and their magnitude
- [ ] Independent contractor vs. employee ratio

### Supply Chain Labor
- [ ] Supplier audit reports (if disclosed)
- [ ] Known supplier labor violations
- [ ] Geographic exposure (high-risk countries)
- [ ] Third-party certifications
- [ ] Forced labor indicators in supply chain

### Worker Voice
- [ ] Glassdoor/Indeed review patterns (not individual reviews)
- [ ] Worker testimony in legal proceedings
- [ ] Union statements
- [ ] Whistleblower allegations
- [ ] Former employee accounts

## Evidence Quality Markers

| Finding | Quality Level | Required Evidence |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| OSHA violation | Verified | OSHA database entry with citation |
| NLRB finding | Verified | NLRB case decision |
| Lawsuit settlement | Verified | Court records or SEC disclosure |
| Forced labor exposure | Credible | CBP WRO or documented investigation |
| Worker safety concern | Credible | Pattern in testimony + supporting data |
| Anti-union activity | Contested | Depends on documentation quality |
| Supply chain violation | Varies | Direct evidence vs. geographic risk |
