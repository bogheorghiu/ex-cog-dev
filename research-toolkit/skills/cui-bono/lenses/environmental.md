# Environmental Lens

## Domain Expertise Required
- Environmental contamination assessment
- Remediation standards and practices
- Environmental regulatory compliance (EPA, state agencies)
- Climate risk and transition exposure
- Environmental justice frameworks
- Indigenous land rights and consultation requirements

## Key Frameworks
- EPA enforcement and Superfund processes
- Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act, RCRA
- NEPA environmental review requirements
- Environmental justice (EJ) screening tools
- Climate disclosure standards (TCFD, SEC Climate Rule)
- Indigenous consultation requirements (FPIC)

## Evidence Sources
- EPA ECHO enforcement database
- Superfund site listings (NPL)
- State environmental agency enforcement databases
- Toxic Release Inventory (TRI)
- SEC climate risk disclosures
- Environmental impact statements
- Academic contamination studies
- Environmental justice mapping tools (EJScreen)
- Community health studies
- Investigative journalism on pollution

---

## Investigation Structure

### 1. CLAIMS
Document entity's stated environmental position:
- Sustainability reports and environmental commitments
- Climate targets and transition plans
- Remediation claims and progress reports
- Environmental certifications (ISO 14001, etc.)
- Community engagement claims

### 2. CONTRADICTIONS

#### A. Direct Search
Search for evidence contradicting environmental claims:
- `[entity name] EPA violation`
- `[entity name] environmental contamination`
- `[entity name] pollution lawsuit`
- `[entity name] Superfund`
- `[entity name] toxic release`
- `[entity name] climate lawsuit`
- `[entity name] environmental justice`
- `[entity name] indigenous land rights`

Check adversarial sources:
- Environmental NGO reports (Sierra Club, Earthjustice, etc.)
- Community group complaints
- Academic contamination studies
- Environmental justice organization assessments
- Indigenous community statements

#### B. Deductive Verification
If entity claims environmental leadership:
- EPA violation rate should be minimal → verify
- No active Superfund liability → verify
- TRI releases should show declining trend → verify
- Community health complaints should be absent → verify
- Climate disclosures should be comprehensive → verify

If entity operates in high-impact sectors:
- Environmental impact assessments should be disclosed → verify
- Remediation progress should be documented → verify
- Climate transition plan should have concrete milestones → verify

#### C. Falsification Attempts
What would PROVE environmental harm?
- Active contamination above regulatory limits
- EPA enforcement actions
- Superfund site responsibility
- Community health impacts documented
- Indigenous land rights violations
- Climate commitments not backed by action

Search actively for these indicators.

### 3. RESOLUTION
Integrate findings into assessment:
- Current contamination: [Documented/Suspected/None Found]
- Legacy contamination: [Significant/Some/Minimal/None]
- Regulatory compliance: [Good/Mixed/Poor]
- Environmental justice concerns: [High/Medium/Low/None]
- Climate transition: [Credible/Questionable/Greenwashing/N/A]

## Specific Investigation Checklist

### Current Contamination
- [ ] EPA ECHO database for recent violations
- [ ] State agency enforcement actions
- [ ] Active permit exceedances
- [ ] Current pollution complaints
- [ ] Self-reported vs. discovered violations

### Legacy Contamination
- [ ] Superfund (NPL) site involvement
- [ ] State equivalent cleanup sites
- [ ] Historical contamination incidents
- [ ] Remediation status and adequacy
- [ ] Outstanding environmental liabilities

### Regulatory Compliance
- [ ] Violation history (type, frequency, severity)
- [ ] Consent decree status and compliance
- [ ] Penalty amounts and patterns
- [ ] Permit status and conditions
- [ ] Self-audit and disclosure practices

### Environmental Justice
- [ ] Facility proximity to marginalized communities (use EJScreen)
- [ ] Disparate impact patterns
- [ ] Community health data near facilities
- [ ] Community consent processes (or lack thereof)
- [ ] Environmental racism allegations

### Indigenous/Community Rights
- [ ] Facilities on or near indigenous lands
- [ ] FPIC (Free, Prior, Informed Consent) processes
- [ ] Treaty rights implications
- [ ] Community benefit agreements
- [ ] Land rights disputes

### Climate Exposure
- [ ] Climate targets and their credibility
- [ ] Transition plan with concrete milestones
- [ ] Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions disclosure
- [ ] Climate litigation exposure
- [ ] Stranded asset risk

## Evidence Quality Markers

| Finding | Quality Level | Required Evidence |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| EPA violation | Verified | EPA ECHO database entry |
| Superfund liability | Verified | NPL listing or PRP notification |
| Contamination above limits | Verified | Regulatory finding or independent testing |
| Community health impact | Credible | Epidemiological study or health agency finding |
| Environmental justice concern | Credible | EJScreen analysis + community testimony |
| Greenwashing | Contested | Gap between claims and verified actions |
| Climate risk | Context | Industry exposure analysis |
