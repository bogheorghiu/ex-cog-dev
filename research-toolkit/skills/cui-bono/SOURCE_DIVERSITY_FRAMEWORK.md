# Multi-Perspective Source Framework — Addendum to Cui Bono & Investigation Skills

> **Purpose**: Explicit guidance for source diversity in geopolitical, financial, and power-structure analysis.
> **Principle**: No source is an authority on reality. Every source reveals something AND conceals something.
> The question is never "is this source biased?" (all are) but "what does this source's position let it see that others can't?"
> **Created**: March 9, 2026 | **For**: cui-bono, deep-investigation-v2, self-improving-investigation

---

## The Framework: Source Positioning, Not Source Ranking

Sources are positioned by two axes:
1. **Proximity to power** — Does the outlet depend on powerful actors for access, funding, or survival?
2. **Geographic/cultural vantage** — What part of the world does this source see FROM?

A source close to power sees internal mechanics but self-censors to maintain access.
A source far from power sees structural patterns but may lack granular detail.
**Use both. Triangulate.**

## Source Categories with Examples

### 1. Independent Investigative (Power-skeptical, documented methodology)

These outlets exist to challenge official narratives. Their bias is toward finding wrongdoing. Use them for: what powerful actors would prefer you didn't know.

| Outlet | Strengths | Watch For |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **The Intercept** | National security, surveillance, military, whistleblower docs | Founded by Omidyar (eBay) — investigate funding when relevant |
| **ProPublica** | Data-driven investigations, government accountability | Nonprofit model, Sandler Foundation funded — transparent but examine |
| **Democracy Now!** | Daily coverage centering affected populations, anti-war | Consistently anti-intervention — strength on military issues, less useful for nuanced geopolitics |
| **Declassified UK** | UK military/intelligence investigations, FOIA-based | UK-focused, limited non-UK coverage |
| **Bellingcat** | OSINT, geolocation, chemical weapons verification | Funded in part by NED (€112K in 2020 per Dutch accounts; NED is Congress-funded but structured as private nonprofit), Dutch Postcode Lottery, and UK FCO-linked Integrity Initiative. Bellingcat acknowledges NED funding but defines it as non-governmental — this definitional reframing (accepting government-origin money while claiming no government funding) is itself analytically significant. Apply scrutiny on Russia/Syria coverage |
| **OCCRP** | Organized crime, corruption, financial networks | Government-funded consortium — transparency is good but note funding |

### 2. Anti-Interventionist / Structural Critique

These outlets challenge the foreign policy consensus. Their bias is against Western military action. Use them for: cui bono analysis of wars, sanctions, regime change.

| Outlet | Strengths | Watch For |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Responsible Statecraft** (Quincy Institute) | Realist IR analysis, anti-intervention, well-sourced | Koch + Soros co-funded — unusual coalition, investigate what that means |
| **Jacobin** | Class analysis, labor, political economy | Explicitly socialist — strength on economic structures, less useful on authoritarian states |
| **Mondoweiss** | Israel-Palestine, US foreign policy in Middle East | Explicitly pro-Palestinian — deep knowledge of the conflict, apply same scrutiny to claims about other actors |
| **Electronic Intifada** | Palestine-focused, long track record | Advocacy journalism — excellent on what it covers, limited scope |
| **WSWS** (World Socialist Web Site) | Class analysis, anti-war, internationalist perspective | Trotskyist — sometimes ideologically rigid, but catches things nobody else does |

### 3. Counter-Narrative / Controversial (Use with extra scrutiny, never as sole source)

These outlets challenge Western narratives but may serve other power structures. Use them for: what the counter-narrative looks like. Always triangulate.

| Outlet | Strengths | Watch For |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **The Grayzone** | US foreign policy critique, sanctions analysis, regime change documentation | Has published credible scoops AND dubious claims. Max Blumenthal's Syria/Xinjiang coverage is contested. **Never sole-source. Always verify independently** |
| **MintPress News** | Military-industrial complex, lobbying, funding flows | Some contributors overlap with state-aligned outlets. Check individual author credibility |
| **RT / TASS** | How Russia frames events, Russian government position | **State media.** Treat as primary source on Russian position, not on facts. Same way you'd treat VOA |
| **Global Times / Xinhua** | How China frames events, Chinese government position | **State media.** Same treatment as RT — reveals Chinese state framing, not independent reality |

### 4. Non-Western / Global South Perspectives

**This is the biggest gap in most analysis.** These outlets see the same events from fundamentally different positions.

| Outlet/Region | Strengths | Watch For |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Al Jazeera** (Qatar) | Middle East depth, Arabic-language sources, ground reporting | Qatar state-funded — excellent on everything except Qatar and GCC internal politics |
| **Middle East Eye** (UK-based, Qatar-linked) | Long-form Middle East analysis | Similar Qatar considerations as AJ |
| **SCMP** (South China Morning Post) | China/Asia from Hong Kong vantage | Alibaba-owned since 2016 — still produces critical journalism but self-censors on some China topics |
| **The Hindu / Indian Express** | India's perspective on global events, Non-Aligned Movement tradition | India's own power dynamics shape coverage — Hindu nationalism influence varies by outlet |
| **Anadolu Agency** (Turkey) | Turkish perspective, bridge between NATO and Middle East | State-affiliated — reveals Turkish state position, useful for understanding NATO's eastern flank |
| **teleSUR** (Latin America) | Latin American left perspective, Venezuela/Bolivia/Cuba vantage | Multi-state funded (Venezuela primary) — reveals Global South framing |
| **Africa Confidential** | African politics and business intelligence | Subscription, UK-based — Western lens on Africa but deep sourcing |
| **Nikkei Asia** | Japanese/East Asian business and geopolitics | Business-focused, establishment-adjacent but high quality |

### 6. Financial/Market (Good Data, Embedded Framing)

| Source | Use For | Watch For |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Bloomberg / Reuters** | Price data, breaking financial news | Framing serves financial industry interests |
| **Goldman Sachs / Morgan Stanley** | Market analysis, scenarios | They are market ACTORS, not observers — their analysis moves markets |
| **Trading Economics** | Macroeconomic data | Data is factual; interpretation is conventional |
| **FT** (Financial Times) | Best financial journalism | Nikkei-owned, establishment but high quality |

## Operational Protocol: How to Use This

### For Any Investigation

1. **Start with at least 3 vantage points**: One Western mainstream, one independent/investigative, one non-Western
2. **For geopolitical events**: Add the perspective of the directly affected region AND the perspective of the opposing side
3. **For power structure analysis**: Prioritize outlets that don't depend on the powerful actors being investigated for access
4. **For financial decisions**: Use financial sources for DATA, non-financial sources for CONTEXT about what the data means

### Red Flags in Source Use

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| All sources from same country/alliance | Echo chamber |
| Only using sources that confirm your thesis | Confirmation bias |
| Dismissing a source because it's "biased" without engaging its evidence | Genetic fallacy |
| Treating ANY source as authoritative | Abdication of critical thinking |
| Using state media as factual source (vs. positional source) | Category error |
| Ignoring a source because it's "controversial" | Missing counter-signal |

### The Core Discipline

**For every claim in every analysis, ask:**
- Who is saying this?
- What is their position relative to power?
- What can they see from their position that others can't?
- What are they structurally unable or unwilling to see?
- Does this claim survive triangulation from a different vantage?

Blind workers should be given sources from DIFFERENT categories to prevent perspective monoculture.
