# Modern Chinese Frame (中文透镜)

## Grammatical Affordances

### Aspect over Tense
Chinese marks HOW an action unfolds, not WHEN. 
- 在...中 (in the process of)
- 了 (completed change)
- 过 (experienced)
- 着 (ongoing state)

**Translation friction:** English forces temporal location. Chinese allows temporal ambiguity with aspectual precision.

### Topic-Comment Structure
Topic first, comment follows. Subject can be absent.
- 这本书，我看过 (This book, I have-read)

**Translation friction:** English requires grammatical subject. Chinese allows topic without agent.

### Verb-Result Compounds
Action and result fused: 看见 (look-see = perceive), 听懂 (listen-understand = comprehend)

**Translation friction:** English separates action from outcome. Chinese fuses them.

## Example

**English:** "Why did the pattern dissolve?"

**中文 processing:**
模式为什么消解了？
(pattern why dissolve-COMPLETED?)

But also possible:
模式在消解中 - pattern in-dissolving-process
模式已经消解过 - pattern already dissolve-EXPERIENCED

**Friction:** English "dissolved" (past tense, done) vs Chinese options for dissolution-as-process, dissolution-as-completed, dissolution-as-experienced. The "why" differently weighted when dissolution is ongoing versus completed.
