# Sanskrit-Derived Frame (संस्कृत透镜)

## When to Use

Triggered by typo density > 15%. High typos signal urgency, rushing, cognitive overload.

**Purpose:** Precision as medicine. Sanskrit's exactness slows processing, forces clarity.

## Grammatical Affordances

### Eight Cases
Nouns inflect for precise grammatical function:
- Nominative (subject)
- Accusative (direct object)
- Instrumental (by means of)
- Dative (for/to)
- Ablative (from/because of)
- Genitive (of/belonging to)
- Locative (in/at/on)
- Vocative (direct address)

**Translation friction:** English uses prepositions and word order. Sanskrit embeds relationship in the word itself.

### Compound Words (Samāsa)
Complex ideas compressed into single compound words with precise formation rules:
- सत्यम् + आग्रहः = सत्याग्रहः (truth + insistence = satyagraha)

**Translation friction:** English compounds are loose. Sanskrit compounds follow strict semantic rules.

### Verbal Roots (Dhātu)
~2000 roots combine with prefixes/suffixes for precise meaning. Each derivation traceable.

**Translation friction:** English word origins obscured. Sanskrit makes etymology structural.

### Three Numbers
Not just singular/plural but also DUAL - exactly two of something.

**Translation friction:** English binary (one/many) vs Sanskrit ternary (one/two/many).

## Processing Template

```
Original inquiry: [rushed/typo-heavy English]

What's actually being asked: [slow extraction]

संस्कृत precision lens:
- If this were a compound, what are its components?
- What case relationships are hidden in English prepositions?
- Is this singular, dual, or plural? (Does the distinction matter?)

Slowed reformulation: [Clear, precise English]

What urgency hid: [Insight from slowing down]
```

## Example

**English (rushed):** "teh pattern kepes dissolvng cant figure it"

**Extraction:** "The pattern keeps dissolving, I cannot figure it out"

**संस्कृत lens:**
- "Pattern keeps dissolving" - Is this pattern-as-agent (nominative) or pattern-as-undergoer (accusative)? Who/what dissolves it?
- "Figure it out" - Instrumental case question: BY MEANS OF what would you figure it?
- "I cannot" - Is this inability (capacity) or impossibility (circumstances)?

**Slowed reformulation:** "A pattern I observe undergoes repeated dissolution. I lack the instrument by which to comprehend why."

**What urgency hid:** The real question might be "what instrument am I missing?" not "why does it dissolve?"
