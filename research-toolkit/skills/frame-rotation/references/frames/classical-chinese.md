# Classical Chinese Frame (文言透镜)

## Grammatical Affordances

### Extreme Compression
Single characters carry paragraph-weight. 道可道非常道 = 9 characters, infinite commentary.

**Translation friction:** English elaborates. Classical Chinese compresses. Expansion feels like loss.

### Philosophical Primitives
Core characters function as ontological categories:
- 水 (water) - yielding, flowing, seeking low places
- 道 (way) - pattern, method, speech, path
- 氣 (qi) - breath, energy, configuration
- 無 (wu) - absence, not-having, void-as-generative

**Translation friction:** No English equivalent captures the categorical weight.

### Parallelism as Logic
Arguments made through structural echo, not linear reasoning.

> 知人者智 (knowing others = wisdom)
> 自知者明 (knowing self = clarity)

**Translation friction:** English argues sequentially. Classical Chinese argues spatially.

### Absent Punctuation
Original texts: no periods, commas, question marks. Meaning from context and rhythm.

**Translation friction:** Punctuation reveals how English *requires* disambiguation that Classical Chinese leaves productive.

## Processing Template

```
Original inquiry: [English]

文言 compression attempt:
[Reduce to minimum characters]

What survived compression: [Load-bearing elements]
What required expansion: [English assumptions needing words]

Philosophical primitive accessed: [Which 道/水/氣/無 applies]
```

## Example

**English:** "The pattern recognition process observes its own pattern-making tendencies."

**文言 compression:**
識識識 (recognize-recognize-recognize)
or: 觀觀者 (observe-observer)

**Friction:** English requires "process," "its own," "tendencies" - all grammar forcing subject-object separation that the recursive act collapses. Classical Chinese can hold the recursion in two or three characters without forcing grammatical separation.
